## Why $500k FDV

The launch FDV is set at what the project can defend with honest early volume — not what it aspires to. At $0.05 per token with 1M circulating, the APY mechanics are immediately compelling on modest daily volume:

| Daily Volume | Annual UNI Rewards | APY (circulating holders) |
|---|---|---|
| $5,000 | $18,250 | ~18% |
| $10,000 | $36,500 | ~37% |
| $30,000 | $109,500 | ~109% |
| $100,000 | $365,000 | ~365% |

> APY = (daily volume × 1% tax × 365) ÷ circulating market cap. Treasury is permanently excluded from rewards, concentrating yield to active holders.

A higher FDV would require proportionally higher volume to produce the same APY. At $2M FDV, delivering 20% APY requires ~$110k/day in volume — a target that cannot be credibly promised at launch. $500k FDV means the token is attractive from day one.

## Yield Mechanics

The 1% transfer tax is collected on every $INDIKIN swap. The accumulated tax is periodically swapped into UNI and distributed proportionally to all circulating holders.

**Key design decisions:**

- Treasury exclusion means 85% of supply generates zero reward pressure, concentrating yield to the 1M circulating tokens. This makes the APY significantly higher per holder than if rewards were distributed across the full supply.
- No emissions. No rebasing. If volume is zero, yield is zero. This is a feature — it means the yield signal is honest.
- Rewards scale linearly with volume. There is no cap, no decay, and no inflation mechanism.

## Hard Rules at TGE

These are written commitments, not guidelines. Every market maker agreement, team agreement, and operational instruction reflects them.

**1. No treasury building for 30 days post-TGE.**  
No open-market token sales from treasury or via market makers for a minimum of 30 days. If operating capital is required in this window, it is funded from pre-raise funds only.

**2. DEX-only at TGE.**  
No centralised exchange listings at launch. Every token not sent to a CEX is sell pressure that does not exist on day one.

**3. Treasury wallet published and monitored from day 1.**  
The multisig address is public and linked from all investor communications, the website, and the pitch deck. Any on-chain activity is visible and will be communicated in advance.

**4. MM inventory = liquidity mandate only.**  
Market makers receive only the token inventory required to fulfil their Uniswap liquidity role. Excess tokens are not provided. Flat retainer structure only — no loan-option arrangements.

**5. SAFT round closes before TGE date is announced.**  
The launch date is not public until anchor SAFT investors are committed. SAFT investors are the institutional demand floor at TGE — they are courted before the date is set, not after.

**6. No airdrop at TGE.**  
Airdrop sell pressure in the first 45 minutes is the leading cause of failed TGEs. If a community distribution is considered, it is sized conservatively and separated from the opening of trading by a minimum of 72 hours.

## Risk Factors

- **Volume dependency.** Yield is entirely volume-driven. If trading volume does not reach targets, APY will be low. This is disclosed clearly to all investors.
- **Circulating supply dilution.** As SAFT and team tokens vest, circulating supply increases and APY per holder decreases at the same volume level. This is modelled and expected — volume growth should outpace unlock pressure.
- **DEX liquidity depth.** With $50k seed liquidity, large trades will experience slippage. Liquidity will be deepened progressively as volume grows and additional capital is raised.
- **Regulatory.** The token is designed for DEX acquisition by retail. MiCA Title II white paper obligations and non-CASP guardrails are under active legal review. No EU retail marketing will commence prior to legal sign-off.
