# $INDIKIN TGE — Go-To-Market Strategy

## My Governing Principle

I will launch at a price I can defend, not a price I aspire to. The UNI rewards mechanism means $INDIKIN gets *more* attractive as volume grows — so I start conservative and let the mechanics prove the case.

---

## 1. Supply — I Control What Hits the Market on Day One

I keep the circulating supply at approximately 1M $INDIKIN at TGE. The remaining 9M stays locked in the treasury multisig — publicly visible, no exceptions.

I launch DEX-only on Uniswap (INDIKIN/ETH). This is not a gap — it is my single biggest structural advantage. Every token I don't send to a CEX is sell pressure I don't manufacture. I will frame this explicitly in all investor and community communications.

Before I set my seed pool price, I stress-test the implied FDV against my honest demand picture — not against aspirational Film3 or DeFi index comps. If the FDV I want requires $100k/day volume to be defensible and I'm launching into $3–5k/day, I price down until the numbers are honest.

---

## 2. Market Making — I Define the Rules Before I Sign Anything

I do not accept the first offer from any market maker. I counter at least twice.

I specify exactly how much inventory the MM needs for Uniswap liquidity management — no more. Excess tokens in MM hands are a short position waiting to happen.

I do not use a loan-option MM structure. I pay a flat retainer.

I write one hard rule into every MM agreement before signing: **no treasury building activity for the first 30 days of trading.** No exceptions. No verbal agreements — written, countersigned.

I also clarify upfront: if the MM holds circulating $INDIKIN, they receive UNI rewards. I need to know whether that creates a conflict before we launch, not after.

---

## 3. FDV — I Launch at What I Can Hold, Not What I Hope For

I benchmark my launch FDV against one question: at $3–5k/day initial volume with a 1% tax, what does the APY look like for circulating holders, and does that make the token worth holding at this price?

If the answer is yes, I have my launch price. I do not add a premium for narrative, vision, or the size of the addressable market. The market does not care about any of those things on day one.

---

## 4. Treasury — I Make the Lockup Visible and Keep It

The 9M treasury wallet address is published on day one and linked from every investor communication, the website, and the Indikin pitch deck. Anyone can verify the multisig is not moving.

I set a written team rule: no open-market token sales from treasury for a minimum of 30 days post-TGE. If we need operating capital in that window, we use pre-raise funds — not the open market.

After day 30, any treasury diversification activity is disclosed in advance to the community with a written rationale. No silent selling.

---

## 5. Institutional Demand — I Build the Buyer Base Before TGE, Not After

The SAFT round closes before I set a TGE date. SAFT investors are not just capital — they are the institutional buying pressure floor at TGE. I need at least one or two anchors committed before I announce a launch date publicly.

I run demo days and roadshow calls with liquid crypto funds in the 60 days before TGE. I treat them as buyers to be courted, not observers who will find us organically if the launch goes well. They will not find us organically. I go to them.

I produce at least one non-paywalled, retail-accessible research brief on $INDIKIN's yield mechanics — written for someone who has never heard of Indikin — and distribute it through crypto-native channels two to three weeks before TGE.

---

## 6. Community and Retail Demand — I Build Something Resistant to Farming

I do not run a generic points program. If I run any pre-TGE engagement campaign, the scoring criteria are not published in full — I manually re-optimize based on observed farming behaviour to surface genuine organic activity.

I invest in localised community building in at least two high-growth crypto adoption regions beyond English-language Twitter. I identify community leads for those regions before TGE.

I do not rely on airdrop recipients as my primary awareness strategy. Airdrop sell pressure in the first 45 minutes is the single most documented cause of failed TGEs. If I do an airdrop, it is sized accordingly and timed to not coincide with the opening of trading.

---

## 7. The One Rule I Do Not Break

I do not convince myself that any of the above does not apply to $INDIKIN because our community is different, or the product speaks for itself, or our tech justifies a higher FDV than the demand picture supports.

The market has no memory of what I built. It only sees the chart in front of it. My job on day one is to make sure that chart gives us tailwinds, not headwinds — because everything after that is easier or harder based entirely on what happens in the first 45 minutes.
