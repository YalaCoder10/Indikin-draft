## Segment: Streaming Consumers
**Internal Draft · March 2026 · Confidential**

## Segment Overview

Streaming consumers are the broadest and most diverse segment Indikin serves. They are people who watch content on streaming platforms — Netflix, Mubi, Amazon, YouTube — and are open to discovering independent productions they cannot find on mainstream services. They may not follow a specific filmmaker, hold any tokens, or care about DeFi. They simply want to watch good films.

This segment is the long-term monetisation engine of IndiFlix. Subscriptions, pay-per-view, and advertising revenues from this audience fund the production fund, sustain the ecosystem, and make the filmmaker token model viable at scale. Without streaming consumers, the platform has no revenue. Without revenue, the production fund has no fuel.

The consumer GTM is distinct from the fan GTM in one critical way: consumers arrive for the content, not the filmmaker. The job is to surface great content, make discovery easy, and convert casual viewers into engaged community members over time. Token participation is never the hook — it is the destination for those who choose to go deeper.

## The Value Proposition

### Why a streaming consumer chooses IndiFlix

| What they get | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Content you can't find elsewhere** | Independent productions from filmmaker-owned catalogues that are not available on mainstream platforms — original, uncompromised storytelling |
| **Support the people who made it** | Every stream on IndiFlix goes directly to the filmmaker and their team. Watching on IndiFlix is an act of support, not just consumption. |
| **Discover filmmakers before they're famous** | IndiFlix is where independent careers are built. Consumers get access to emerging voices before mainstream platforms acquire them. |
| **No algorithm suppression** | Content is discoverable on merit. No pay-to-play promotion, no studio-backed content crowding out independent voices. |
| **Flexible access** | Pay per view for single productions or subscribe for full library access. No forced annual commitment. |
| **Optional deeper participation** | If a consumer loves a filmmaker, they can choose to hold their token, earn streaming revenue, and participate in their community — but none of this is required to watch. |

### The honest pitch to consumers

Mainstream streaming platforms pay independent filmmakers almost nothing. When you watch on Netflix or Amazon, the money goes to the platform, not the people who made the film you love. IndiFlix is a streaming platform where watching a film is a direct act of support for the person who made it. The content is here because the filmmaker chose to share it here — because this platform actually pays them.

## Target Audience Profile

### Primary: Independent film enthusiasts
People who actively seek out independent, documentary, and art-house content. They subscribe to Mubi, attend festivals, and are frustrated by mainstream platforms' lack of independent content.

- **Profile:** 25–55, educated, culturally engaged, film literate
- **Platform behaviour:** Mubi, MUBI GO, BFI Player, Curzon Home Cinema, documentary streaming services
- **Motivation:** content quality, filmmaker discovery, support for independent culture
- **Geography:** UK, US, Australia, continental Europe (particularly Scandinavia, France, Germany)
- **Reachable via:** Letterboxd, independent film media, film festival newsletters, Mubi community

### Secondary: Creator economy audience
People who already follow independent creators on YouTube, Patreon, or Substack and are comfortable paying directly for content from individuals rather than platforms. They understand the economics of creator support.

- **Profile:** 20–40, digital native, already pays for multiple content subscriptions
- **Platform behaviour:** Patreon supporter, YouTube Premium, newsletter subscriber
- **Motivation:** direct creator support, access to content that wouldn't exist on mainstream platforms
- **Reachable via:** creator economy newsletters, YouTube communities, Twitter/X

### Tertiary: Casual independent film browsers
People who watch mainstream streaming platforms but occasionally seek out independent content when recommended. They are not actively looking for IndiFlix but would use it if they discovered compelling content.

- **Profile:** 25–50, watches Netflix/Amazon primarily, open to alternatives
- **Motivation:** content recommendation from trusted source
- **Reachable via:** word of mouth from primary segment, social media discovery, mainstream entertainment media

## Channels

### 1. Content as the acquisition channel
The single most effective consumer acquisition tool is the content itself. When a film on IndiFlix is excellent and discussed, it drives people to the platform. The GTM strategy for consumers starts with ensuring the content catalogue is strong enough to earn word-of-mouth.

This means the filmmaker GTM is upstream of the consumer GTM. More filmmakers, better content, stronger catalogue, more consumers. The sequencing is non-negotiable.

**Actions:**
- Curate launch catalogue — do not open IndiFlix to consumers until there are at least 30 quality productions available
- Homepage features 5–8 flagship productions that represent the best of what Indikin filmmakers have produced
- New content calendar: at least 2 new productions added per week post-launch
- MODRA ensures content quality standards — the platform's reputation depends on what it hosts

### 2. Letterboxd
Letterboxd is the most important consumer acquisition channel for independent film. 15 million film lovers, highly engaged, trust each other's recommendations, actively seek out independent and under-seen films.

**Actions:**
- Every Indikin production listed on Letterboxd with a direct IndiFlix streaming link
- All Indikin filmmaker profiles on Letterboxd, linked to IndiFlix
- MARKA runs a Letterboxd engagement strategy: responding to reviews, adding productions to curated lists, building Indikin presence as a trusted indie platform
- Target 50+ Letterboxd user ratings per production within 3 months of launch
- Letterboxd list: "Stream Direct — Films Where Your Money Reaches the Filmmaker" — position Indikin productions as a category

### 3. Film media and criticism
Independent film consumers read about film. They follow critics, read reviews, and discover content through editorial recommendations. Earned media in film publications drives high-quality, high-intent traffic.

**Target publications:**
- Little White Lies — reviews and features; UK-based, international readership
- Sight & Sound — prestige, highly trusted by the primary audience
- Mubi Notebook — online editorial; documentary and art-house focus
- RogerEbert.com — strong US independent film audience
- The Guardian Film section — broad but film-literate readership
- Indiewire — essential for US independent film consumers
- Documentary Magazine — documentary audience specifically

**Actions:**
- Pitch individual productions to critics for review — not the platform, the films
- Filmmaker interview features in film media — the human story drives consumer interest
- "Where to watch independent film" editorial placements — IndiFlix positioned as a destination
- ANALYXA tracks referral traffic from film media to measure channel effectiveness

### 4. Social media — content discovery
Consumer social media strategy is content-first, filmmaker-story-driven, and platform-agnostic in tone. The goal is discovery, not brand loyalty.

**Platform priorities:**

**YouTube** — long-form filmmaker interviews, "making of" content, production stories. Consumers who discover a filmmaker's story on YouTube then seek out their films.
- Target: 2 filmmaker interview videos per month
- Format: 10–20 minutes, authentic conversation, no production polish required

**Instagram** — visual storytelling, filmmaker portraits, film stills, behind-the-scenes. High organic reach for film aesthetics.
- Target: daily story content, 4 feed posts per week
- Format: film photography aesthetic — not corporate, not crypto

**TikTok** — short filmmaker story clips, "did you know this filmmaker" format, film trivia tied to IndiFlix productions.
- Target: 5 posts per week
- Format: 30–60 seconds, hook in first 3 seconds, filmmaker as character

**Twitter/X** — film criticism commentary, industry news, filmmaker spotlights.
- Target: 3–5 posts per day
- Format: cultural commentary + Indikin content links

### 5. Streaming platform partnerships
Third-party streaming platforms that carry independent content can become distribution and referral partners for IndiFlix — either through licensing deals or cross-promotion arrangements.

**Target partners:**
- Mubi — potential licensing partner for Indikin filmmaker productions
- BFI Player — UK market, documentary and independent film focus
- Fandor — US independent streaming
- Plex — ad-supported free tier, broad reach
- Tubi — free ad-supported, growing independent film catalogue

**Actions:**
- Outreach to Mubi and BFI Player for licensing discussions — positions Indikin as a content supplier, not just a competitor
- Licensing revenue is a secondary benefit; the primary value is distribution reach and consumer discovery
- All licensing agreements include "originally available on IndiFlix" attribution

### 6. Podcast advertising and sponsorship
Independent film, documentary, and culture podcasts reach the primary audience in a high-attention environment. Podcast listeners have above-average willingness to pay for quality content.

**Target podcasts:**
- The Treatment (KCRW) — in-depth filmmaker and artist interviews
- Frame by Frame (BBC) — UK film podcast
- Little Gold Men (Vanity Fair) — awards season but broad film audience
- Doc Soup (Hot Docs) — documentary audience specifically
- Film at Lincoln Center podcast
- Filmmaker Magazine podcast

**Actions:**
- Sponsorship deals with 2–3 podcasts in the first 6 months
- Host-read ads only — authenticity matters to this audience
- Promotional code for IndiFlix trial linked to each podcast
- Filmmaker guest appearances where possible — the filmmaker as spokesperson is more compelling than brand advertising

## Messaging Framework

### Core narrative
*Independent films worth watching. Made by people who own them. Watched by people who want to support them. IndiFlix — stream direct.*

### Messaging by audience type

**For independent film enthusiasts:**
- Lead with: content quality and filmmaker ownership
- "The films you can't find anywhere else — watched in a way that actually pays the people who made them"
- Film-first language — no crypto, no tokens, no blockchain
- Tone: film critic to film lover — intelligent, discerning, culturally engaged

**For creator economy audience:**
- Lead with: direct creator support model
- "Like Patreon, but for watching films. Your subscription goes to the filmmaker, not a platform."
- Bridge from existing creator support behaviour — they already do this for podcasters and writers
- Tone: peer-to-peer, values-aligned, anti-platform

**For casual browsers:**
- Lead with: content recommendation from trusted source
- "Your friend told you to watch this. You can watch it on IndiFlix."
- Friction-free access — pay per view, no subscription required for first watch
- Tone: simple, inviting, no barrier to entry

### Key messages by objection

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already pay for too many streaming services" | Pay per view from £2.99. No subscription required. Watch one film, see if you like the platform. |
| "I've never heard of these filmmakers" | Neither had anyone before they watched. That's the point — IndiFlix is where you discover careers before they're mainstream. |
| "Is this a crypto thing?" | IndiFlix is a streaming platform. You watch films, you pay the filmmaker. The blockchain is infrastructure in the background — you never interact with it. |
| "Why not just use YouTube?" | YouTube pays creators fractions of a penny per view. IndiFlix pays filmmakers properly because the platform is designed to do that, not designed to sell advertising. |
| "What's the content quality like?" | 53 productions already live, curated from 16 filmmakers. Quality is the platform's reason for existing — we'd rather have 10 excellent films than 1,000 mediocre ones. |

## Consumer Onboarding Journey

### Step 1: Discovery
Consumer discovers IndiFlix through film media, Letterboxd, social media, filmmaker community, or word of mouth.

### Step 2: First watch
Frictionless pay-per-view option — no account required for first interaction. Lowest possible barrier to first experience.

### Step 3: Account creation
Post-first-watch prompt: create an account to track watchlist, get filmmaker updates, and access the full catalogue.

### Step 4: Membership upgrade
Email sequence introduces membership tiers — framed as "more access, more connection to the filmmakers you're watching."

### Step 5: Community discovery
Pro and Star Pass members introduced to filmmaker communities — optional, never forced.

### Step 6: Token participation (optional)
For members who want to go deeper, token participation is introduced as an extension of their existing support — "become an investor in the films you love."

## Dependency: Consumer GTM Depends on Filmmaker GTM

This section exists to be explicit about a structural dependency that must be understood by the whole team.

**Consumer acquisition cannot scale before the content catalogue is strong enough to warrant it.** Spending marketing budget to drive consumers to a platform with 15 productions is wasteful and damaging — first impressions determine whether a consumer ever returns.

The sequencing is:
1. Filmmaker onboarding → catalogue growth
2. Content quality threshold met (30+ productions, 5+ flagship)
3. Consumer acquisition begins at scale
4. Consumer revenue feeds production fund
5. Production fund attracts more filmmakers
6. More filmmakers grow the catalogue
7. Catalogue growth drives consumer acquisition

Marketing budget for this segment should not be deployed at full capacity until the filmmaker onboarding programme has delivered a catalogue worth promoting.

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Catalogue too thin at launch to retain consumers | Hard minimum of 30 productions before consumer marketing begins; quality over speed |
| Consumers associate platform with crypto and disengage | Film-first messaging in all consumer-facing channels; no blockchain language; IndiFlix is a streaming platform |
| Pay-per-view friction deters first-time users | Guest checkout option; no account required for first watch; frictionless as possible |
| Competition from Mubi, BFI Player, and Curzon | These are potential partners as much as competitors; niche is filmmaker ownership and direct support, not catalogue breadth |
| Content discovery is hard without algorithm investment | Letterboxd integration, film media reviews, and filmmaker community channels substitute for algorithmic discovery in the early phase |
| Revenue growth slower than projected | Consumer revenue is one of seven revenue streams; ecosystem does not depend on this single channel performing to plan in year one |
