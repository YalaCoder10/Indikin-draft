## Key Observations

**Mid-market pricing reaches near break-even in Y1.** At $9/$25/$49/$149, revenue and costs are almost exactly matched in year one ($966,800 vs $967,000). This is the strongest investor narrative — the business is not burning cash from day one, the raise is being deployed into growth and acquisitions rather than covering operating losses.

**Acquisitions are the Y2–Y3 growth engine.** Seven targets at $120–180k average annual revenue each contribute $600k–$1.26M per year by Y3. This is the most operationally aggressive assumption in the model and should be presented to investors with appropriate caveats around integration timelines.

**Legal costs front-load then normalise.** $170k in Y1 drops to $90k by Y3. Investors should understand this is structural — the Cayman Foundation, MiCA compliance, and SAFT legal are one-time setup costs, not recurring overhead.

**The agentic org model is a structural cost advantage.** Keeping headcount to 5 humans while scaling revenue to $7M+ (mid-market Y3) produces margins that conventional media or SaaS businesses at this stage cannot match.

**Token APY is a retention and acquisition mechanic, not operating revenue.** The UNI rewards pool shown in revenue tables demonstrates ecosystem health. As daily volume grows from $8k to $60k, holder APY grows from 58% to 438% — this is the primary reason to hold $INDIKIN and the mechanism that drives secondary market volume.

## Risk Factors

- **Member growth dependency.** The model assumes 2,500 paying members in Y1 — a figure that requires a functional product, active filmmaker content, and meaningful marketing spend from day one of the raise.
- **Acquisition integration speed.** Seven acquisitions in three years is operationally aggressive. Delays in integration push acquisition revenue into later years and increase integration costs.
- **Token volume.** All APY projections are volume-dependent. If $INDIKIN trading volume underperforms, holder yield falls and token demand softens — creating a potential negative feedback loop.
- **Pricing elasticity.** The mid-market pricing scenario has not been market-tested. ARPU assumptions should be validated against comparable Film3 and indie streaming platforms before being presented as projections.
- **Regulatory.** MiCA Title II obligations and non-CASP compliance are under active legal review. Delays in legal sign-off could defer EU retail marketing and suppress Y1 member growth.
